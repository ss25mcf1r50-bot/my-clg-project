HISTORY

The National Institute of Technology (NIT) Warangal, established in 1959 by Pandit Jawaharlal Nehru as the first Regional Engineering College, holds the distinction of being the pioneer among India's NITs. It was elevated to the status of a Deemed University in 2002 and later designated as an Institute of National Importance under the name National Institute of Technology, Warangal. NIT Warangal is renowned for its strong emphasis on research, innovation, experienced faculty, state-of-the-art infrastructure, and a global alumni network. Key Milestones 1959: Foundation stone laid by Pandit Jawaharlal Nehru on October 10, 1959, marking the establishment of Regional Engineering College, Warangal. 2002: Achieved status as a Deemed University, renamed National Institute of Technology, Warangal. 2004: Held its first Convocation. 2018: Celebrated its Diamond Jubilee, a testament to its six decades of service to the nation. Historical Context The college was established as part of a plan by Pandit Jawaharlal Nehru to promote technical advancement and national integration through the Regional Engineering Colleges. REC Warangal was the first in a chain of 31 such institutes that were eventually upgraded to NITs. Initially affiliated with Osmania University (1959), it later came under the purview of Jawaharlal Nehru Technological University (1972), gaining academic autonomy. It was then affiliated with Kakatiya University in 1976 before achieving its own university status. Present Status Today, NIT Warangal stands as a premier engineering and technology institution in India. The institute offers a wide range of undergraduate, postgraduate, and doctoral programs across various fields. It is recognized for its commitment to research, innovation, entrepreneurship, and providing technological services to the community. About Us - NIT Warangal October 10, 1959.
